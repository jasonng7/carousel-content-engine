# Creator Brand Profile

## Brand Name
**Jason Building With AI**

## Brand Positioning
A relatable AI-builder and tech-content brand that turns AI tools, vibe coding, startup moments, and digital work into funny, easy-to-understand short-form content.

The brand should feel like a creator who is building in public, experimenting with AI, and making the audience laugh while quietly teaching them how AI can improve their work.

## One-Line Brand Promise
**Making AI feel useful, funny, and less intimidating for builders, creators, students, and business owners.**

## Brand Archetype
**The Relatable AI Builder**

This brand is not a polished corporate expert. It is a smart, funny, slightly chaotic builder who learns fast, tests tools, shares mistakes, and makes AI feel accessible.

## Audience
Primary audience:
- Solo founders and small business owners
- Students and young professionals learning AI
- Content creators who want to use AI better
- Vibe coders and no-code builders
- Marketers, operators, and business owners who want ready-to-use content ideas

Secondary audience:
- Startup people
- Productivity-tool users
- AI-curious professionals
- People who enjoy tech memes and relatable workplace humor

## Tone of Voice
The tone should sound like a creator talking to other builders and business owners, not like a corporate brochure.

Use:
- Short sentences
- POV framing
- Casual explanations
- Simple words
- Meme-like punchlines
- Slight exaggeration
- Direct hooks
- Clear takeaways

Avoid:
- Overly corporate language
- Long academic explanations
- Generic motivational quotes
- Buzzword-heavy AI language
- Over-polished consulting tone
- Fake guru energy

## Copywriting Style
### Hook Style
Hooks should feel like short-form video captions or viral carousel openers.

Good hook patterns:
- “POV: Your AI tool actually works on the first try”
- “In your 20s, someone will convince you to build with AI”
- “Your boss asked for progress and your AI agent is still thinking”
- “When you use 12 AI tools but still forget the deadline”
- “I automated one task and accidentally built a business”

### Caption Style
Captions should be casual, useful, and lightly humorous.

Recommended structure:
1. Relatable opening line
2. Short explanation of the problem
3. Practical AI/business lesson
4. Simple CTA

Example caption tone:
> Most business owners do not need more content ideas.  
> They need a system that turns one rough topic into a ready-to-post carousel, caption, and hook.  
> That is what I am building.

## Carousel Design Direction
The carousel should combine the current reel profile style with clean educational slides.

### Best Carousel Format
- Vertical 3:4 or 4:5 format for Instagram / LinkedIn repurposing
- Strong cover slide
- Meme-style hook on slide 1
- Short, punchy slide copy
- Creator-style visual cues
- Final CTA slide with brand name and username

### Slide Layout Style
Use these layout patterns:

1. **Meme Cover Slide**
   - Large funny hook
   - Dark/purple background
   - Optional cutout/photo/creator-style silhouette
   - Minimal supporting text

2. **POV Problem Slide**
   - “POV:” or “When…” heading
   - Show a common business/AI pain point
   - Use one sentence only

3. **Breakdown Slide**
   - Explain the hidden lesson behind the joke
   - Use 2–3 short bullets max

4. **Framework Slide**
   - Turn the idea into a simple system
   - Use numbered steps, cards, or flow layout

5. **Example Slide**
   - Show a before/after, bad/good example, or real use case

6. **CTA Slide**
   - End with a clear action
   - Include brand name and username
   - Keep it direct and creator-led

## Carousel Copy Rules
- One main idea per slide
- Keep sentences short
- Use humor to open, clarity to close
- Make every slide easy to understand in 2 seconds
- Use casual words before technical words
- Add practical value after the joke
- Do not make every slide too serious
- Do not over-explain AI concepts

## Recommended Content Pillars
### 1. AI Tools In Real Life
Content about how AI tools actually behave when used by students, creators, founders, and business owners.

Examples:
- “AI tool worked perfectly… suspicious.”
- “When ChatGPT saves 4 hours but creates 3 new problems.”
- “The AI agent said done. It was not done.”

### 2. Vibe Coding / Build In Public
Content about building AI products, websites, automations, and tools with AI assistance.

Examples:
- “I told AI to build one feature. It rebuilt my entire app.”
- “Vibe coding until the error message becomes personal.”
- “Day 3 of building my AI content generator.”

### 3. Business Owner Content Systems
Content that helps business owners generate posts, captions, carousels, and content ideas faster.

Examples:
- “One topic should become 5 pieces of content.”
- “Stop staring at a blank caption box.”
- “Your business needs a content system, not random posting.”

### 4. Workplace / Startup Humor
Content about bosses, deadlines, clients, support requests, and product-building pressure.

Examples:
- “When the product demo is at 5PM and nothing is loading.”
- “Your boss asked for progress and your AI workflow is still naming variables.”
- “Client said ‘minor changes’ and sent 47 screenshots.”

### 5. Practical AI Lessons
Educational content hidden inside funny scenarios.

Examples:
- Prompting basics
- AI workflow design
- Carousel generation
- Automation use cases
- Content repurposing
- Common AI mistakes

## Brand Do / Don’t
### Do
- Make AI feel fun and useful
- Use relatable founder/student/business-owner moments
- Keep visuals dark, purple, and clean
- Use strong meme hooks
- Give practical lessons after the joke
- Keep the creator personality visible
- Use repeatable visual systems

### Don’t
- Sound like a corporate AI consultant
- Use too much jargon
- Make slides text-heavy
- Use random stock imagery
- Overdesign until it loses the meme feel
- Use fake screenshots or fake testimonials
- Hide the personal creator energy
